Finding Mukteshwar

Journey to the Sacred

Reached through winding forest paths in the Deogarh Forests of Odisha, where the journey itself becomes a passage inward.

Location

A Place of Stillness and Grace

Nestled amid dense forests and quiet hill roads, the Mukteshwar Temple is located in a serene and secluded stretch of eastern India. Reached through winding forest paths, the temple stands as a place of stillness and grace, where nature, silence, and devotion come together.

For many, the journey itself feels like a gentle passage inward, preparing the seeker for the sacred space that awaits.

Getting There

Directions

From Sambalpur (Closest Railway Station)

The Mukteshwar Temple is located approximately 56 km east of Sambalpur. From Sambalpur town, the drive takes about 1.5 to 2 hours, passing through Jamankira and scenic forested stretches along NH53. The final approach involves smaller interior roads, offering a quiet and contemplative journey to the temple.

From Jharsuguda (Closest Airport)

Jharsuguda lies about 50 km west of Sambalpur. Visitors traveling from Jharsuguda can first drive to Sambalpur via NH49 routes, and then continue eastward toward Mukteshwar. The total travel time from Jharsuguda is approximately 2.5 to 3 hours by road, depending on traffic and road conditions.

Travel Guidance

Preparing for Your Journey

🗺️

Navigation

GPS coordinates: 21.561689°N, 84.366575°E. Download offline maps as mobile connectivity may be limited in remote areas.

🎒

What to Bring

Simple, modest clothing, personal medicine, basic toiletries. The temple provides meals and basic accommodation essentials.

🌦️

Best Time to Visit

October to March offers pleasant weather. Monsoon season (June-September) brings lush beauty but challenging travel conditions.

📞

Communication

Inform someone of your travel plans. Keep emergency contacts handy. The temple can assist with local arrangements if notified in advance.
