Karma Yatra

A Month of Sacred Living

Immerse yourself in the timeless rhythm of temple life. This is not a retreat — it is a returning to what you have always been.

What is Karma Yatra

Dwell in the Sacred

A Karma Yatra at Mukteshwar is not a structured program or workshop — it is an opportunity to live in communion with an ancient spiritual tradition. For one month, you become part of the temple community, participating in daily worship, contributing through seva (selfless service), and allowing the profound stillness of this sacred place to work its transformation upon you.

There is no curriculum here, no teacher except the space itself. The temple, the lake, the ancient trees, and the rhythm of daily practice become your guides. You will discover what it means to live simply, to serve without expectation, and to sit in meditation not as a technique, but as a natural expression of being.

This is an invitation for those serious about inner work — those ready to step away from the noise of modern life and listen to what emerges in genuine silence.

The Experience

Elements of Temple Life

🕉

Temple Life

Participate in morning and evening puja. Witness the ancient rituals that have continued unbroken for centuries. The temple opens at dawn — be there.

🧘

Silent Sitting

The temple's energy supports deep meditation naturally. Sit by the lake, beneath the ancient trees, or in the sanctum. Let stillness find you.

🌿

Simple Living

Vegetarian meals. Minimal possessions. Early mornings. The simplicity is not a sacrifice — it is the practice itself. Less becomes infinitely more.

🙏

Seva

Contribute to the temple's upkeep and the community. Cleaning, cooking, gardening — every act becomes an offering when done with awareness.

Living Guidelines

Life in the Dham

Sacred Observances

  • Vegetarian food only — no meat, fish, eggs
  • No alcohol, smoking, or intoxicants
  • Modest, appropriate dress at all times
  • Early wake (4:30 AM) and early sleep
  • Participate actively in seva (service)
  • Maintain silence in temple areas
  • Mobile phones discouraged, silent when necessary
  • Respect for the sacred space and community

Sadhana Guidelines

  • Morning meditation by the lake
  • Evening meditation in the temple
  • Personal practice in your room
  • No formal instruction provided
  • The space itself is the teacher
  • Honor your own rhythm and needs
  • Practice presence in all activities
  • Allow transformation to happen naturally
